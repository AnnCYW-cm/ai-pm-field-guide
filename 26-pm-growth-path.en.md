## Part 1: Workflow

### The 4 Independent Actions of Personal Native (No Company Required)

This is the most important line in the finale — **4 things a PM can start without waiting for any company resources**.

#### Action 1: Run 1 prompt experiment with Claude Code every day

See Article 17.

- Tool: Claude Code / Cursor / ChatGPT (any)
- No company account needed, pay $20/month yourself
- 30 minutes per day
- Validate whether next week's proposed requirements are feasible

After 3 months — **you're 10x stronger than 90% of PMs who only write PRDs at their companies.**

#### Action 2: Read 50 traces from any product

See Articles 20 and 21.

- Tool: any AI product that exposes traces (ChatGPT / Notion AI / Lovable / Cursor)
- Doesn't have to be your company's product
- 50 per day
- Train your "AI product pathology" instinct

After 6 months — you can look at an AI product's output and immediately classify which type of bad case it is.

#### Action 3: Write a PRD with eval cases

See Article 12.

- Tool: any doc tool (Notion / Markdown / Feishu)
- Doesn't need company approval, write one for yourself
- Pick any product requirement from your company
- Equip it with 50 eval cases + 4-tier bad case grading + a cost card

When you job-hop in 3 months — this PRD is your strongest resume.

#### Action 4: Handle 100 real bad cases

See Article 21.

- Source: while using ChatGPT yourself, note every bad case + write "how I'd fix it"
- No company permission needed, pure personal training
- Accumulate 100 → you're already in the top 10% of PMs who understand bad case management

**Core**: These 4 actions **don't require your company to have any AI infrastructure**. You can start tonight.

### 4-Tier Self-Assessment (Personal Native Progress Scale)

Each tier corresponds to different market scarcity. Identify which tier you're at — it determines how fast you should move next.

| Tier | Core Skill | Personal Native Action Completion | Market Scarcity |
|------|-----------|----------------------------------|-----------------|

| L2 · AI Product Participant | Writes PRD with eval + reads traces | Actions 1-3 all done | Medium (under-supplied) |
| L3 · AI Product Architect | Can build eval system + make downgrade decisions + brief boss on industry frameworks | All 4 actions + led 100+ bad cases | High (very rare) |
| L4 · AI-Era Product Thinker | Defines "what makes a good AI product" + writes industry-level product notes | L3 + sustained public output 2+ years | Extremely high (<100 globally) |

#### L1 → L2 key actions
1. Use AI tools for at least 30% of daily work
2. Write a PRD with eval cases (even if no one asked)
3. Build 3 product prototypes with Claude Code

Time: 3-6 months

#### L2 → L3 key actions

4. Brief your boss on "why we shouldn't ship AI here" at least once
5. Read 1000+ traces

Time: 1-2 years

#### L3 → L4 key actions
1. Write 1 industry-influencing product note
2. Lead 1 AI product from 0 to 1 (million-dollar ARR)
3. Consult for industry leaders on AI products
4. Sustain public output (newsletter / talks / book) for 2+ years

Time: 3-5 years

## Part 2: Engineering Reality

### The 4 Stages of Company Native (Helping Your Boss See the Path)

PMs sprint on the fast personal track — but the company is on the slow track, and PMs still need to push the boss forward.

See Article 25 for AI Native Team Building. Compressed CEO-alignment version here:

#### Stage 1 (1-3 months): Pilot
- Pick 1 team (e.g., customer support) to arm with AI
- No layoffs — let the team use AI tools
- Observe productivity changes + customer satisfaction

#### Stage 2 (3-6 months): Expansion
- Push pilot lessons to 2-3 teams
- Hire 1 Eval Engineer
- Get PMs and engineers using Claude Code / Cursor

#### Stage 3 (6-12 months): Org Reshaping
- Hire AI Ops for monitoring
- Transition QA team into Evaluators
- When business expands, prioritize AI-powered productivity over headcount

#### Stage 4 (12-18 months): Maturity
- Whole company runs on "Human × AI = 5-10x productivity"
- Per-capita ARR significantly above competitors

**Key**: This roadmap has **no "layoff" step** — see Klarna counter-example in Article 25.

### Why the Two Tracks Must Be Separated

Many PMs bundle "company transformation" and "personal growth" together — this is the biggest cognitive misalignment in the AI era.

| Dimension | Company Native (Slow Track) | Personal Native (Fast Track) |
|-----------|----------------------------|-------------------------------|
| Time | 1-2 years to start, 3-5 years to mature | Start today, L3 in 3 years |
| Control | 90% in boss's hands | 100% in yours |
| Dependencies | Budget, HR, compliance, culture | Just $20/month Claude subscription |
| Risk | Slow, passive, may stall | Fully autonomous |
| Reward | Company transforms → you benefit | Personal L3 → 5-10x job-hop leverage |
| Decoupling | Company doesn't transform → your skills unaffected | Personal transforms → 3-5 years ahead of company |

**The most critical judgment** —

**If your company is on the fast track** (already shipping AI products) — both tracks run in parallel, company provides production scenarios, your personal skill jumps to L2-L3 directly.

**If your company is on the slow track** (hasn't started AI transformation) — you MUST raise personal Native priority **to the top** — in 3 years when you job-hop, you'll have 5-10x leverage over peers.

**The most dangerous state** — company doesn't transform, you don't either, just waiting for the wind — in 3 years you have neither company platform nor personal skill, **this is the biggest failure mode for AI-era PMs**.

### 3 "Company-Independent" Growth Patterns Observed in the Wild

Repeatedly observed industry pattern — 3 types of PMs use this playbook to land offers with significantly above-peer leverage within 1-2 years:

#### Pattern 1: Traditional SaaS → AI Application Layer
- **Starting point**: 5-year PM at a traditional SaaS company that hasn't started AI transformation
- **Personal actions**: 1 hour/day on Claude Code prompt experiments + write portfolio of PRDs with evals + sustain public output (Xiaohongshu (RED) / Jike / X) for 12+ months
- **Job-hop outcome**: Salary leverage typically 1.5-2.5x range (per industry recruiter signals, varies by region + stage)

#### Pattern 2: B2B Tools → Production-Grade AI Startup
- **Starting point**: Mid-level PM at B2B tools shop, has used Claude Code / Cursor for internal prototypes
- **Personal actions**: Led at least 1 full pre-launch eval (5 categories, see Article 19) + handled 100+ real bad cases + briefed boss on downgrade decisions (see Article 22)
- **Job-hop outcome**: Direct L3 role at AI startup (see L3 definition — AI Product Architect)

#### Pattern 3: QA / Test Architect → AI PM
- **Starting point**: QA / test architect background, no traditional PM experience
- **Personal actions**: Wrote PRD with FMEA lens (see Article 11 PRD: 4 Adds, 2 Cuts) + led eval system buildout + applied QE mindset to probabilistic AI systems
- **Job-hop outcome**: Direct L2-L3 AI PM role, **QE background becomes the differentiation anchor** (this is the author's own path — 8 years QA → AI PM)

### What the 3 Patterns Have in Common

Observe across all 3 — 3 commonalities:

1. **Don't wait for company to provide AI projects** — personal subscription + $20/month is enough to start
2. **Use "1 hour/day × 12 months" to accumulate a verifiable portfolio** — not consuming courses, producing artifacts
3. **Public thought leadership output** — let recruiters find you, not the other way around

### The Recruiting Signal

Industry recruiter signal observed repeatedly —

> **Candidates with AI PM portfolio in hand command significantly higher leverage than those who can only say "my company didn't let me do AI."**

Why? Because AI companies know —
- "Company didn't let you" ≠ "you can't"
- Portfolio = verifiable evidence of capability
- Public thought = verifiable evidence of judgment

**This is a stable 2025-2026 industry pattern** — not an exception, it's the reward mechanism for Personal Native.

### The PM's Position in the AGI Countdown — What Happens in 18-24 Months

The two tracks are explained — but before closing, one final layer of perspective must be raised. **A fact this season can't avoid**: Dario Amodei wrote in his January 2026 essay *The Adolescence of Technology* — "powerful AI arrives in 2026-2027, and 2027 may see a 'country of geniuses in a datacenter' (50M agents each smarter than a Nobel laureate, running at 10-100x human speed)."

Whether or not this timeline is exact — **as a PM, you must plan around AGI being 18-24 months away**. Dario could be wrong by 1-2 years, but the framing can no longer be "AGI is 10 years away."

This means a fundamental shift in PM work —

**PRD exits, Agent Spec enters**. Mike Krieger has already compressed PRDs into 3-paragraph Product Notes. The next step is **writing Agent Specs for sub-agent fleets to consume** — input / output / rubric / fallback / escalation path, every field agent-executable.

**The PM's value anchor shifts from "knowing users" to "knowing models + knowing orchestration"**. When 50M agents are smarter than Nobel laureates — "knowing users" is something agents do better than PMs themselves. The PM's irreplaceable value becomes **"I know better than the agent which combination of agents should be orchestrated to solve this user problem."**

**The PM's work shifts from "chasing schedules" to "commanding Agent teams"**. OKRs become agent rubrics, 1-on-1s become trace + outcome review, quarterly retros become "which agent workflows are worth solidifying into skills for next season."

This season's 26 articles are the current toolkit. But after reading, **keep one thing in mind** — all the methodologies in this book have shelf lives. L2 toolkits (PRD / eval / bad case / Claude Code) have 12-18 months shelf life, L3 judgment (orchestration / downgrade / boundaries) has 24-36 months, L4 vision (what makes a good AI product) has 5+ years.

The higher up — the longer the shelf life, the higher the return. That's why Personal Native must **invest at all three layers simultaneously**, not just patch L2.
